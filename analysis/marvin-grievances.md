# Marvin Heemeyer's Grievances

Source scope: this file summarizes Marvin's grievances as he presents them in the tape transcripts. It does not verify the truth of any allegation. The operative phrase throughout is "Marvin says" or "Marvin believes."

## Core Grievances

| Grievance | Marvin's version of the harm | Primary named targets | Source |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| FDIC auction resentment | Marvin says he bought the Granby property legally at auction, but Cody Docheff, local power brokers, and the town treated him as an interloper because Cody had wanted the property back. | Cody Docheff, Gus Harris, local leaders, Town of Granby | Tape 1 Side A, Tape 2 Side A |
| Retaliation for buying the property | Marvin believes the community interpreted his auction purchase as a personal offense and then spent years trying to punish him or force him out. | Docheffs, Thompsons, Town of Granby, "good old boy" network | Tape 2 Side A, Tape 3 Side A |
| Water and sewer denial | Marvin says the 1992 water and sanitation process kept him from hooking onto utilities by invoking a maintenance easement across Gus Harris's land. He later believes this was deliberate interference. | Water and sanitation board, Bud Wilson, Dick/Ron/Larry Thompson, Randy Schmuck | Tape 1 Side A, Tape 3 Side B |
| Altered or sanitized minutes | Marvin says the 1992 sanitation meeting minutes omitted the maintenance easement discussion he remembers, which he interprets as record manipulation. | Water and sanitation district, local officials | Tape 1 Side B, Tape 3 Side A |
| Lost rental and retirement income | Marvin says denial of utilities prevented him from developing rental units, subdividing, renting or selling the muffler shop, and building the retirement income he expected. | Sanitation district, Thompsons, Town of Granby | Tape 1 Side A, Tape 2 Side A, Tape 3 Side B |
| Illegal or improper spot zoning | Marvin says the town had earlier spot-zoned Gus Harris's two acres from HGB to industrial, creating the bridge that would later support a concrete plant. | Town of Granby, Gus Harris, Cody Docheff, Thompsons | Tape 2 Side A |
| Concrete plant relocation into town | Marvin says Cody could have kept the concrete plant elsewhere, including near the gravel pit or Hot Sulphur Springs, but moved it near Marvin's property out of malice. | Cody Docheff, Docheff family, Town of Granby | Tape 1 Side A, Tape 2 Side A, Tape 2 Side B |
| Dust, noise, and property impairment | Marvin says the concrete plant harmed his property, business, future rentals, motel neighbors, and the town environment through dust, noise, and industrial activity. | Cody Docheff, Town of Granby, planning and town boards | Tape 2 Side A, Tape 3 Side A |
| Building permit as pre-approval | Marvin says the town issued a permit for a "water storage tank" while the PDO process was still pending, which he sees as proof the concrete plant was effectively pre-approved. | Town of Granby, Cody Docheff | Tape 1 Side B |
| PDO procedural maneuvering | Marvin says the town had missed public hearings, then used Resolution 2000-9-12B to reframe a final hearing as a preliminary stage and keep the project alive. | Town of Granby, town attorney, Rich Daly, Docheff interests | Tape 1 Side B, Tape 4 Side A |
| Newspaper hostility | Marvin says Patrick Brower and the local paper were hostile to him, favored the concrete plant, denied him helpful publicity, and framed the conflict against him. | Patrick Brower, Sky-Hi News | Tape 1 Side B, Tape 3 Side A |
| Junk ticketing and selective enforcement | Marvin points to the September 7, 2000 newspaper item about Mountain View Muffler being ticketed for junk while the concrete plant foundation was nearby. He reads this as selective pressure. | Town of Granby, local newspaper | Tape 1 Side B |
| Legal system failure | Marvin says attorney Dietze took his money, encouraged him to keep fighting, failed to act at the most effective time, and refused to appeal. | Dietze, Judge Doucette, local legal system | Tape 1 Side B, Tape 2 Side B |
| Judicial conflict | Marvin says Judge Doucette had previously opposed a concrete plant near his own property and should not have decided Marvin's case. | Judge Doucette | Tape 2 Side B |
| Refusal to appeal | Marvin says Dietze would not appeal because of future cases before Judge Doucette, which Marvin treats as personal self-protection at Marvin's expense. | Dietze | Tape 2 Side B |
| Failure to offer a way out | Marvin says local leaders should have "thrown him a bone" or bought him out so he could leave, but instead they exploited the fact that he wanted out. | Town board, planning commission, Docheffs, neighbors | Tape 2 Side B |
| Sale and auction shortfall | Marvin says he tried to sell for about $600,000, later reduced the price, and eventually netted about $400,000. He treats the difference, plus lost business value, as stolen retirement wealth. | Docheffs, town leadership, potential local buyers | Tape 2 Side B, Tape 3 Side A |
| Community snickering and humiliation | Marvin says council members and others snickered at him after he lost, deepening his view that they enjoyed his defeat. | Town council, Casey Farrell, local community members | Tape 2 Side B |
| Bribery or side deals | Marvin says the Broken Arrow motel owner was bribed or bought off through a building deal with Cody during the process. This is Marvin's allegation, not a verified finding. | Broken Arrow owner, Cody Docheff | Tape 3 Side A |
| "Good old boy" system | Marvin frames the larger grievance as a closed local network that keeps outsiders down, bends rules, changes minutes, uses secret meetings, and protects insiders. | Town of Granby, planning commission, town board, Docheffs, Thompsons, neighbors | Tape 2 Side A, Tape 2 Side B, Tape 3 Side A |
